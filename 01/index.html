RO.ME "3 DREAMS OF BLACK"

Rome was a super interesting browser-based interactive movie / music video! It's great to see developers collaborating with artsts in this way, and it seems like it was a great strategy for bringing some attention / showing off what WebGL can do.

Technology: The technologies used in the project were WebGL and THREE.js. It's interesting that THREE.js never gets mentioned in the tech video, but you can see in the console and in some of the B-Roll in the video that they're using THREE.js.

Comparison: My favorite other intractive story was dillasdonuts.shop! It's a site that showcases an album that I really like by an artist who has long since passed. It's a great way of preserving his legacy, and it also just looks really cool. Like RO.ME, it uses THREE.js, but has more of a traditionaly website user flow, with different pages to view different songs off of Dilla's album.
